Agile

This is not going to be a history of Agile. Agile is a methodology (or process) by which software is developed. The idea is pretty simple. Work is broken down into small chucks and put in a `product backlog`. Then the development team (or `scrum team`) decided what can be done in a certain amount of time and starts working. That unit of time is called a `sprint`.

There is a concept, that you will hear the mentors say to each other, called `MVP`. In this case, it doesn't mean that we are watching for the best developer among you. We are trying to find the Minimum Viable Product, or the simplest thing we can build to get the job done. It means keep it simple on the first version.

We are going to do a VERY simple version of Agile with sprints lasting only a few hours.

At the end of a sprint, we send our work to `production`.

Let's walk through an example.

We are going to build a piece of software that powers a time machine. So we have our agile board:

Backlog In Sprint Done Production
- Build Flux Capacitor
- Purchase Delorean
- Travel to 1955

The sprint team will discuss what can be done in a sprint and who is going to work on it. For this sprint, they decide they can handle the first two `user stores'. They decide to assign the Flux Capacitor to Doc Brown and Marty will get the Delorean. Now our sprint board looks like this:

Backlog In Sprint Done Production
- Travel to 1955
 - Build Flux Capacitor (Doc Brown)
- Purchase Delorean (Marty McFly)

Since this movie is only a few hours long, we will need to keep our sprints short. So 30 minutes, later Doc slips on a toilet seat, hits his head and invents the Flux Capacitor. This is not my recommended development style.

Backlog In Sprint Done Production
- Travel to 1955
 - Purchase Delorean (Marty McFly)
 - Build Flux Capacitor (Doc Brown)

10 minutes later Marty drives up in the all aluminum Delorean. They are finished with development. They will go to production under the instruction of their team leader. We will discuss the actual way to move to Prod in Step 5.

Backlog In Sprint Done Production
- Travel to 1955
 - Build Flux Capacitor (Doc Brown)
- Purchase Delorean (Marty McFly)

Now we are ready to start the next sprint. Since the last user story is pretty big, we decide to put two developers on it and start development.

Backlog In Sprint Done Production
- Travel to 1955 (Doc Brown & Marty McFly)
 - Build Flux Capacitor (Doc Brown)
- Purchase Delorean (Marty McFly)

You'll have to watch the movie to see if they finish the last sprint and ship the product on time or if they get fired and replaced with lower cost labor from Wakanda.
